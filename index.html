Our Mission

At Bridge Housing our mission is to provide affordable housing projects around the city. The goal of our project is to create a website exploring abandoned buildings and other vacant areas in the Twin Cities area (in particular St. Paul) and display how they could be repurposed into affordable housing.

Facts

St.Paul is filled with many areas that are currently abandoned/underdeveloped. According to the St. Paul public housing agency, over 50% of all renter households in St. Paul pay more than 30% of their income on housing.

Projects

A prominent project in St. Paul that takes advantage of vacancies is the recent project in the Highland Park neighborhood to repurpose the old Ford plant. The Ford plant was opened in 1925 and after almost 90 years of operation it was closed in 2011. After years of attempted environmental redevelopment, there is currently a plan in place for the old Ford plant site.

The plan for the Ford plant involves the creation of 3,800 housing units, 265,000 square feet of office space, and 150,000 square feet of retail space. 760 of the housing units will be affordable to middle income families. Although the allocation of resources could be more equitable to lead to more affordable housing, this represents an important shift towards creating more affordable housing in the city of St. Paul.

Proposal for more projects that can be undertaken in order to expand affordable housing by dealing with vacancies. A prominent abandoned building in the St. Paul area is the abandoned Hamm’s brewery.
